Animals

Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) is a powerful, social big cat known for its majestic mane and role as a top predator in the African savanna. Living in prides, lions rely on cooperative hunting to catch prey. Male lions protect the pride's territory, while females are the primary hunters.

Tiger

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is a solitary, powerful big cat distinguished by its striking orange coat with black stripes. As an apex predator, it primarily inhabits forests and grasslands in Asia, hunting large prey like deer and wild boar. Tigers are known for their strength, stealth, and swimming ability.

Cheetah

The cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus) is the fastest land animal, capable of reaching speeds up to 70 mph. It has a slender body, distinctive black "tear marks," and primarily hunts during the day in open savannas. Unlike other big cats, cheetahs rely on their incredible speed to catch prey.

Elephant

The elephant is the largest land animal, known for its massive size, long trunk, and large ears. Elephants are highly social and intelligent, living in matriarchal herds in diverse habitats across Africa and Asia. They use their trunks for various tasks, including feeding, drinking, and communication.

Zeebra

The zebra is a herbivorous mammal known for its distinctive black-and-white striped coat. Native to Africa, zebras live in herds and are highly social, relying on their stripes for camouflage and social bonding. They primarily graze on grasses and migrate in search of food and water.

Horse

The horse (Equus ferus caballus) is a large, strong mammal known for its speed, endurance, and versatility. Domesticated for thousands of years, horses have been used for transportation, work, and sport. They are social animals that often form close bonds with humans and other horses.
