Petra

Petra is an archaeological site in Jordan, famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system.
Established around the 5th century BCE as the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom, it features the iconic
Treasury and Monastery structures. Often called the "Rose City" due to the color of the stone, Petra is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is a towering statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, standing 30 meters
tall atop the Corcovado Mountain. Completed in 1931, the statue is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone,
symbolizing peace and welcoming visitors with open arms. It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and an iconic landmark of Brazil.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Incan city set high in the Andes Mountains of Peru. Known for its sophisticated
dry-stone construction and terracing, it was built as an estate for the Inca emperor Pachacuti. Often
called the "Lost City of the Incas," it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza is a large Mayan archaeological site in Mexico, featuring the iconic pyramid El Castillo.
Built between the 9th and 12th centuries, it served as a major political and economic center for the Maya
civilization. The site, renowned for its architectural and astronomical significance, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the
New Seven Wonders of the World.

Roman Colosseum

The Roman Colosseum, an iconic symbol of ancient Rome, is a massive amphitheater built between 70-80 AD
under the emperors Vespasian and Titus. Originally capable of seating around 50,000 spectators, it hosted
gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and mock sea battles. Despite its partial ruin due to earthquakes and stone robbers, it
remains a testament to Roman engineering and architectural prowess.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan
between 1632-1653 in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Renowned for its architectural beauty, it features
intricate inlay work and symmetrical gardens, symbolizing eternal love and paradise. The Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage
Site and one of the most iconic structures in the world.
